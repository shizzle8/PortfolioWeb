EEG - Analysis of Sleeping/ Active Patterns

Previous Next

Electroencephalogram (EEG) is a recording of the electrical activity of the brain from the scalp. In other words, it is the registration of the neural activities of the brain in the form of brain waves. EEG data can be used to control a piece of hardware in real-time using Arduino microcontroller and OpenBCI kit. This is done with the help of electrodes which are specifically placed at Frontal Parietal 1 and 2 as well as Parietal 3 and 4 to extract the correct data required to successfully complete the investigation. As with anything, EEG is susceptible to interference from various sources. These can be physiological or non-physiological. This interference (noice) can be filtered with a simple notch filter. Finally, we used an LCD display to show facial expressions and a fan with the use of an Arduino to test whether the user is relaxed (Theta waves) or focused (Alpha and beta waves).
